Powerful Prime Minister's Portrait Part 2

2020-07-25
Word count: 876   |   Reading time≈ 5 min

Positive part of the powerful controversial Prime Minister.

His Comity

People usually see Zhang Juzheng’s rebelliousness, but in fact, he is sometimes gentle and frugal. Lu Shusheng, a well-known noble-minded litterateur at the time, was the top in the Jiajing Twenty Years (1541) examination and awarded as sergeant. Later being Shujishi (Short-term positions in the Imperial Academy), he was also an editor. Lu personally was resigned to draw up 12 chapters of academic rules and regulations for Guozijian, the highest school of Ming Dynasty, and taught all students, finally gaining outstanding success and good official reputations. Lu Shusheng was so indifferent to fame and fortune that refused the appointment of the court to him as cabinet minister several times. After Zhang Juzheng took charge of the administration, he valued the talent and prestige of Lu, and personally went to invite him to be the head of the Minister of Rites with humble attitude. One day, when Lu Shusheng went to the cabinet to handle official duties, he was unhappy with his slightly misplaced seat and refused to take a seat. Zhang Juzheng understood him, and quickly gave up the main seat to Lu to get him satisfied.

Lu Shusheng later quarreled with the eunuch and resigned. Zhang Juzheng personally paid a visit persuade him stay, sighing to Lu’s little brother: “The most virtuous official in the imperial court is your brother.” But Shusheng was so determined to leave, and Zhang could do nothing. Nevertheless, Zhang Juzheng asked him to recommend his successor — Wan Shihe, the director of the Ministry of Rites, who was kept in Juzheng’s mind. After Lu returned to his hometown, Zhang Juzheng also apologized to him: “As the prime minister, it is pity for me to fail to persuade you, a highly-respected minister, to stay.” Despite the failure to keep Lu Shusheng in Imperial court, he won Lu’s understanding. Later, when Zhang Juzheng’s encountered “sieving love”, and all ministers were accusing him, as the leader of the noble-minded litterateur, even though held disapprove of Zhang’s violation of the etiquette, Lu understood Zhang’s difficulties on serving a country in his heart. Lu had a recall with Sun Yuefeng, the ambassador of the court: “In the past when discussing state affairs with Zhang, he said that he would avoided complaining, and be willing to blame for every mistake.” No matter how others criticized him or scolded him, he will always be satisfactory. During the Song Dynasty, the prime minister handled government affairs in the political affairs hall, sitting and being greeted by hundreds of officials. In the Ming Dynasty, there was neither a prime minister nor a political affairs hall, and those secretary of the Grand Secretariats used the court room as an office. Ordinary officials stood up and consulted political affairs. Only the leader of Dali Temple (leader of the Supreme Court), Lu Guangzu dared to speak to Zhang Juzheng and changed this rule. At that time, Zhang was in charge of the country overwhelmingly. He usually talked with the guest, simply and clearly, in a few short sentences. Lu Guangzu suggested: “Daily affairs should be discussed in detail, and I’d like to request a seat before I can do my best. Otherwise, I will retire and no longer expect prosperity.” Once participating in the same Imperial exam and wining the top two, Lu Guangzu are familiar with the allusions of the country and are shrewd and capable. Ju Zheng respected him and accepted his proposal happily and set up a seat for consulting officials. Since then, this rule was settled and ordinary officials and cabinet ministers talked in the court room with seats.

A Diligent Person

Zhang Juzheng’s dedication and hard work is recognized by all, and not except for his political opponents. Zou Yuanbiao, a minister against “seizing love” and punished by Zhang Juzheng, said in his later years: “He always focus on country affairs as long as he is alive.” Day and night, he had to rush to the cabinet to handle government affairs before dawn until the midnight. No any other prime ministers can go beyond such professionalism. His student Wang Zudi said in the sacrificial article for him: “As a prime minister, he aged rapidly for worrying about the country.” Although looked suspicious, he was more healthy and hard-working. He looked like a eighty-year old man while he was less than sixty, from which one can imagine how hard he worked in his old age. Furthermore, Zhang Juzheng is not only diligent in politics and cares about the country, but also meticulous. During the Chongzhen period, the head of the Ministry of Rites Huang Jingfang’s “Guo Shi Wei Yi” (A book to explore the gains and losses of the political chaos of the dynasties and make fair judgments), mentioned that Zhang Juzheng once self-supported “explorer” who specialized in inquiring and collecting news about the border. He knows the situation of border better than the governor of Border. So they never dared to deceive him. From those facts, we can see in the late 16th century, peace in the Chinese border and people-living is attributed to the great politician Zhang Juzheng.
