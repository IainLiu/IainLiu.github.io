Powerful Prime Minister's Portrait Part 1

2020-07-11
Word count: 874   |   Reading time≈ 5 min

Zhang Juzheng was a famous prime minister of the Ming Dynasty. He propped up the precarious Ming Empire lonely, and tried to save the decline since Zhengde and Jiajing. Finally, with his unremitting efforts, he kept the social stability and economic prosperity of the late Ming for decades, making the Wanli dynasty a peaceful and prosperous era cherished by scholar-officials in the late Ming and early Qing dynasties. On the other hand, his arrogant, resolute style of acting and his narrow-mind, stubborn character have attracted hundreds of years of infamy.

As the most controversial powerful minister in the Ming Dynasty, for four hundred years, we believed that “the praise word may be exaggerated, and destroy may lose the truth.” Zhang Juzheng, first of all, is a person, who has struggled and conflicts throughout his life, while with both pour soul and human kindness. He has both dignity that no need to be angry but still gives people pressure with his temperament and humorous wit; He has the arrogance in self-respect, but a humble and comity democratic style; He can be the pioneer to reform the government even with the cautiousness of skating on thin ice in case of heading into danger. Now I tried to through the layers of history, looking for clues from the vast history books, and restoring a true and complete Zhang Juzheng.

An Unruly People

Both friends and political opponents of Zhang said that Zhang Juzheng was a text book man of proud and insolent because of his talents. Zhang Juzheng is a visionary politician and a practical doer. However, he is not sympathetic to others with a high self-esteem. The six central departments can only obey his decisions internally and externally. Although secondary grand secretaries, Lv Tiaoyang, Ma Ziqiang and Zhang Siwei, have worked with him for many years, they can do nothing but to obey. When the emperor was young, everything was arranged by Zhang Juzheng, leading to his biggest power and supremacy so that all depended on his wink.

In Ming Dynasty, when the imperial court gave orders, officials had to take the orders at the south bank of Jinshui Bridge at Meridian Gate, but Zhang Juzheng sat in the cabinet and sent people to receive orders on his behalf, which caused all kinds of criticism. With his ignorance of all these criticisms, others could do nothing. In the second year of Longqing (1568), Xu Jie, the teacher of Zhang Juzheng, also the senior grand secretary (prime minister) retired. For Xu Jie, Zhang Juzheng always kept the disciple ceremony, while to other cabinet ministers, he did pay no attention. For example, the secondary grand secretary Li Chunfang, was the No.1 in one common imperial examination with him also with two-year cabinet experience earlier than Zhang. So Zhang Juzheng was behind him in terms of ranking in scientific research and political qualifications. On hearing of Xu Jie’s retirement after being slandered by rumors, Li Chunfang felt a sense of sorrow and grieve and sighed: “A virtuous prime minister like Xu can’t protect himself. How long can I stay here? “He replied him without hesitance impolitely, “Yes, you should go home for the elderly. Only in this way can you preserve your good reputation.” Li Chunfang was very surprised when and soon resigned from office and returned to native town. As we can see, he is quite obvious and arrogant.

In September of the fifth year of Wanli (1577), Zhang Juzheng’s father passed away, which push Zhang Juzheng into an extreme dilemma when the news reached him. According to the etiquette, parents passing away requires returning home regardless of one’s official position. At the time when the Emperor is young and the political situation is not stable, the court can’t live without Zhang Juzheng. Li Youzi, director of the Bureau of Revenue, advocated the idea of ​​”seizing love”(When one sacrifices filial piety for the country, he can just work with plain clothes and participate in no auspicious gifts without quiting the job), and they decided to keep Zhang Juzheng in the imperial administration. The cabinet Lv Tiaoyang and Zhang Siwei quoted the story of Yang Pu, Jin Youzi, and Li Xian from the previous dynasty, begging to retain Zhang Juzheng. Emperor Shenzong immediately made an order, asking him to “Share my sorrow, don’t be overly sad,” and after the 49th day of death, he needed to continue to work in the cabinet. Zhang Juzheng’s beg for burying his parent was not allowed, so he needed to take off the mourning clothes and put on normal clothes, which caused a sensation in the court. Many officials was convicted of impeaching him “forgetting relatives and being greedy for positions” and “brazen”. Wang Xijue, a bachelor of the Hanlin Academy visited Zhang Juzheng hurriedly and interceded for those convicted officials. Zhang Juzheng, with “bows down angrily and held a knife against his neck”, yelling: “The emperor begs me to stay, but you all force me to leave. What should I do? Why not just kill me!” History describes this plot in a mocking tone, and it is this incident that reflects Zhang Juzheng’s toughness and no compromises.
