The "Silly" Emperor Part 1

2020-03-13
Word count: 966   |   Reading time≈ 6 min

Li Zhi, the son of Emperor Taizong of the Tang Dynasty, or the man who let his wife usurp the throne of his family, was the flicker of light in the light of his father’s prominent reign and the backdrop panel of his grandson’s “Age of Kai Yuan”. His generosity was said to be cowardice, and his understatement was treated as mediocrity. But in reality, the real him was as good as any of the promising emperors in history.

# From a showpiece to a full-fledged emperor in power #

The game of imperial power and prime ministerial power

1.ZHANGSUN WUji, who made imperial power a figurehead

Before the sixth year of Yong Hui, the young Emperor Li Zhi found that the supreme power of the Tang Empire was not in the hands of the emperor, but in the hands of the chief prime minister, the entrusted official appointed by his father who severed three emperors, and his own maternal uncle, Zhangsun Wuji. He had little or no say in the affairs of the state, whether in making decisions, in the appointment and dismissal of personnel, or in the imperial family affairs of establishing a Crown Prince.

Li Zhi was very frustrated and helpless: his crown prince title was nominally contested by his uncle, and he was also crowned by his uncle who had been “working hard” for his family since the founding of the Tang Dynasty…. …From this point of view, he seemed need be grateful to his uncle. But when he thought about it, the Tang Empire belonged to the Li family, but it looked like Zhangsun was taking over control. Moreover, Zhangsun Wuji not only dominated the court, but also interfered in Li Zhi’s family affairs, which was breaking LI Zhi’s bottom line little by little.

At last, the famous case “Fang Yiai Rebellion” that followed made Li Zhi unable to sit still. At the end of the fourth year of Yong Hui, an ordinary family dispute caused by two sons of Fang Xuanling, another prominent founding minster and entrusted official was used by Zhangsun Wuji as a tool for political cleansing.

Among the nine members of the Chancellor’s team, except for LI Ji who supported Li zhi and a weathercock Yu Zhining, the other six, Chu Suiliang, Han Ai, Lai Ji and Cui Dunli, etc., were all in cahoots with Zhangsun. Although Zhansun Wuji was only greedy for power and had no intention to abolish the regime or to usurp like Wang Mang, the influence of the ministerial power on the emperor’s power was already unbearable to Li Zhi.

*2. Li Zhi’s political counterattack *

The two are never allowed to walk side by side on the road to power, much less imperial power. Li Zhi was generous but not stupid, and politically he has inherited strong genes from his father. So, when he found that he already had some power, he began to fight back.

Step 1, the abolition of the Empress

This was the first real game between Li Zhi and the ZHANGSUN Group, and Li Zhi said that he could and should decide who would be his wife. The ZHANGSUN Group said the Empress had no faults and could not be deposed. The two sides did not give way to each other, but Li Ji joined in which was a turnaround for LI Zhi, who was on the losing end..

In October of the sixth year of Yong Hui, Empress Wang was deposed by an edict from LI Zhi for the following reasons.

On the nineteenth of that month, according to the joint petition of hundreds of officials, Emperor Gao Zong, LI Zhi, crowned WU Zetian as Empress. This was the first time Li Zhi exercised his imperial power.

Step 2, beak through the Iron Curtain

The so-called “Iron Curtain” was an intricate web of official connections that had been built up by ZHANGSUN WUji over the years inside and outside the imperial court. Within this network, the power of the ZHANGSUN group was spread to all corners of the empire. Every appointment and order of the emperor was deeply marked with ZHANGSUN WUji’s mark. The situation was first torn apart by the Empress’s abolition - in September of the sixth year of the Yong Hui dynasty, a month before the abolition, ZHANGSUN WUji’s best friend, the equally veteran CHU Suiliang, was demoted from the court, and the trumpet of doom for the ZHANGSUN clique began to sound.

Step 3, the abolition and made of the Crown Prince

In the first month of the first year of Xian Qing (656 A.D.), LI Zhong, a prince championed by the ZHANGSUN clique, was deposed as king of Liang, and LI Hong (born to the Empress WU), who was only four years old, was made Crown Prince, causing ZHANGSUN to retreat wretchedly.

Step 4, the downfall of ZHANGSUN

In the fourth year of Xian Qing, a so-called WEI Jifang and LI Chao clique case occurred in the eastern capital of Luoyang that had no relations with Zhangsun WUji. However, under the direct orders of LI Zhi, the case not only turned into a rebellion, but also featured ZHANGSUN WUji as the protagonist—the same as the one who had framed WU King, LI Ke, and others a few years earlier.

The Old Tang History—Biography of ZHANGSUN WUji recorded that “The Emperor (LI Zhi) did not even ask WUji the reason for his rebellion, but listened to (Xu) Jingzong’s false construction.”

In the end, ZHANGSUN WUji failed, who had been an official for more than thirty years. In this game of imperial power versus prime ministerial power, LI Zhi was victorious.

But was the power in his hands really safe?
