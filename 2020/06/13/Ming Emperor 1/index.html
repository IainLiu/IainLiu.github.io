Weird Emperor's Meaningful Life Part 1

2020-06-13
Word count: 902   |   Reading time≈ 5 min

The Ming emperors were very “weird”, and Zhu Houzhao was one of them. It is said that he was unrestrained and love freedom, preferred the market to the palace, and once posed as a merchant in the palace, shouting prices; he was also addicted to pleasure, alcohol and sex, and trusted eunuchs ……. But it was such a so-called “fatuous ruler”, who was able to reform the taxation of the south of China, kill the eunuch Liu Jin in a snap of his fingers, and even marched in person to beat the invading Mongolia prince. In addition, Zhu Houzhao was gifted in Buddhism and Islam, and was also the first Chinese emperor who could speak the language of Western Europe.

He is a misunderstood emperor who appears to be a “fatuous ruler”, but is actually An emperor with personality. He was unconventional in small matters, but never confused in big matters. Let’s take a look at this controversial and legendary emperor and find out who he really was.

In the fifth year of Hong Zhi (1492), Emperor Wu Zong came to the throne. ZHU Houzhao was just a 15-year-old boy who became the emperor overnight. He would never be described as a wise and sage emperor. The emperor’s greatest fear was that he would be too fatuous to use eunuchs, but whether he was fatuous or not is open to debate, but his preference for eunuchs over scholars is clear.

At that time, Emperor WU Zong favored 8 eunuchs in total, led by Liu Jin, who was called the “Eight Party” or “Eight Tigers”. In order to curry favor with the Emperor, the “Eight Tigers” would present the emperor with strange and bizarre things every day, and often organized various performances and sports activities. Like his peers, the young emperor was fond of all kinds of entertainment, either playing the role of a vaudeville actor in the palace, or leading eunuchs to sneak into the streets and enjoy the strange. Sometimes, he dressed himself as a merchant, wearing a hat, holding a scale, and shouting his prices with an abacus. In the fouth year of Zheng De (1509), EMperor Wu Zong had a strong interest in the Mongols, he produced a large number of felt hats and leather furs, mobilized a palace of people to wear and to play Tartar to amuse each other. With many Mongolians gallop, he often did not return at night, staied in civilian homes and behaved and ordinary people.

Although Emperor Wu Zong favored eunuchs, it did not have any serious consequences. One of the reasons why Emperor Wu Zong was able to eliminate the eunuch LIU Jin at the snap of his fingers was that Liu Jin did not hold military power, which means that Emperor Wu Zong was not a faint-hearted ruler who favored eunuchs and did not care about the government. Although he was having fun with the eunuchs, he was clear-headed, sober, and always on the lookout for eunuchs. As to why Wu Zong favored eunuchs, it may have been to balance the power of the civil officials, which were further expanded and far more powerful than the military officials after the defeat of Emperor Zhu Qizhen in 1449 at Tumu Fort (in Huailai County, Hebei Province). The civil rulers lowered taxes on the large landowners and instead imposed heavy taxes on the peasants. In addition, they deliberately misinterpreted the state policy, extending the subsidies and encouragement limited to outstanding literati to the entire scholar and gentleman class, reducing or even exempting them from taxes, thus annexing land and amassing wealth, and increasingly eroding the foundation of the state. As a child, Wu Zong was very clever, so how could he not understand the complicated relationship between these interests? Naturally, he didn’t want to make the same mistake and become a puppet of the literary group like the previous emperor.

Wu Zong has been criticized for the construction of the Leopard Palace. Why is the Leopard Palace a dark history of the Emperor Wu Zong, was it too much work for the people and a waste of money? According to testimony, the Leopard Palace covered an area of only 4 hectares, and costed about 20 million silver. Compared to the Old Summer Palace and the Summer Palace with the cost of thousands or even millions of millions of silver, it was only a drop in the bucket. In fact, the focus of the problem lies in the fact that many people believe that the Leopard Palace as a lust place was where Zhu Houzhao had fun day and night. It is said that in the Leopard Palace, songs, dance, games, wine, women were all available, and the Emperor was indulgent in them with the abandonment of government affairs, which caused the people’s grievances. However, it was also believed that the Leopard Palace was not a place to play, but rather a place for him to live and deal with affairs of state. Other scholars later pointed out that traditional historical sources had scandalized and misrepresented Emperor Wu Zong, suggesting that the move was an attempt to restore a lifestyle and political atmosphere that had disappeared since the early 16th century. Keeping leopards for hunting was an attempt to restore the military power of the Ming dynasty and to show the courageous style of the emperor.
