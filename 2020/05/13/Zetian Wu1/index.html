Different Sides of the Ever Queen Part 1

2020-05-13
Word count: 903   |   Reading time≈ 5 min

WU Zetian entered the palace at the age of fourteen. After succeeding to the throne of Tang Gaozong, she was made Empress in the sixth year of Yong Hui (655) and gradually assumed power. By the first year of the reign of Emperor Tian Shuo (690), WU Zetian changed the name of the country to Zhou and became the first female emperor in Chinese history. In modern literature, films and TV shows, WU Zetian is often portrayed as a “tyrant”, a woman who seduced the emperor with her beauty, and a “tyrant” with her power and decisiveness. Numerous works have focused on the number of righteous people killed by her cruel politics. However, few people know that she is also regarded as a great politician who succeeded the reign of Zhen Guan and laid the foundation for the reign of Kaiyuan.

Innovate the imperial examination system and make good use of people.

The imperial examination system was an important system for selecting officials in ancient times. WU Zetian inherited the policy of the imperial examination system from Emperor Taizong of the Tang Dynasty on the one hand, and reformed and innovated the system on the other, making it more complete.

WU Zetian relied on the Jinshi subject that focusing on analyzing instead of the scripture subject, and took poetry and literature talent as the focus of assessment, which changed the situation that the children of noble families dominated, and opened up the way for the children of poor families to enter the public service, making the imperial examination system a real channel for selecting talents. The people who passed the test were called “Jinshi.” During the reign of Emperor Taizong of Tang Dynasty, there were 205 Jinshi, but during the reigns of Emperor Gaozong and Emperor WU Zetian, there were more than 1,000 Jinshi. During the reign of Emperor Xuanzong of the Tang Dynasty, Yao Chong, Song Jing, Zhang Jiuling, and literary giants such as Chen Zi’ang and Liu Zhiji were all outstanding talents selected through the imperial examination system. In the first year of the reign of Emperor WU Zetian (690), she held a large-scale imperial examination at the Luocheng Hall in Chang’an, the capital city, and there were tens of thousands of candidates. In addition, WU Zetian also created the Women’s Examination System and the Martial Examination System, which provided opportunities for women and martials to enter the imperial court and participate in political affairs.

In addition to the imperial examinations, nominations were also an important way for WU Zetian to discover talents. Those who passed the examinations were given official central government posts. This also gave rise to the “testing official” system. Xue Jichang, who was a commoner, was rewarded by WU Zetian for his political opinions and became a powerful inspector. He punished General Hou Weixu, who lied about the military situation, and killed the corrupt county captain WU Ze. He was applauded by the people as an able official.

The story went that Shangguan Wan’er, who was implicated by her grandfather Shangguan Yi when she was born, was sent to the palace as a maid. At the age of fourteen, she was already a rising star with great talent and ability. Despite being an enemy’s offspring, WU Zetian valued her talent and put her in charge of the palace. Cen Xi was demoted because of his involvement with the prime minister, Cen Changqian, and was never reappointed. It was not until 704 A.D. that Prime Minister Wei Zili recommended Cen Xi, who had been implicated by his uncle, to WU Zetian, who said, “How can someone so talented be dragged down all the time!” Cen Xi was appointed as a member of the staff. Once this precedent was set, many people who had been implicated in the past but were talented were reactivated.

In addition to this, WU Zetian also introduced the system of “self- recommendation”. In order to encourage test officials and other newly appointed officials to be faithful to their duties and not humiliate their mission, WU Zetian often admonished them before they took up their posts to encourage their march. To the new governor and other three grades above civil and military officials, WU Zetian gave each embroidered robe to encourage, embroidered robe tattooed “You must be clear in your governance, and your orders must be fair. You must be honest, prudent, loyal and diligent, respectful of your superiors and close to the people.” From this, we can see that WU Zetian attached great importance to talents, enlightened thinking and effective measures.

WU Zetian appointed 78 prime ministers, including LI Zhaode, SU Liangsi, WEI Yuanzhong, DU Xingjian, DI Renjie, ZHANG Xianzhi, YAO Chong, SONG Jing, etc., and the border generals included TANG Tijing, LOU Shide, HEIJIA Changzhi, GUO Yuanzhen, etc. These civil and military generals played important roles in the political stability and economic and cultural development of the Tang Dynasty. Therefore, later people said, “the present prosperous relays on knowing people’s wisdom, and the dynasty relays on the use of talented people”. In her later years, she promoted Yao Chong, Song Jing and Zhang Jiuling, who all became famous prime ministers by the time of Emperor Xuanzong of the Tang Dynasty. She was good at employing people and making good use of capable people, which was one of the achievements of WU Zetian’s administration.
