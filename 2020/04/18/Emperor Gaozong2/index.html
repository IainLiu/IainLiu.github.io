The "Silly" Emperor Part 2

2020-04-18
Word count: 1.2k   |   Reading time≈ 7 min

Continue the last passage of Emperor Gaozong. This passage mainly focus on his unknown achievements.

The gradual loss of royal power

The Tang couple’s shop, “The Two Saints’ Court”

When you live at home, is your wife close or your friends close? Of course it is the wife. Because wives are family anyway, and friends, no matter how close, are theoretically outsiders - so thought LI Zhi, the third leader of the Great Tang.

It began by an accident. In April, in the fifth year of Xian Qing, ZHHANGSUN WUji fell to his death and LI Zhi came into power. But his spontaneity lasted only half a year, and in October, the turning point for him, or the Tang Dynasty, came—he had a stroke. Although he was not partially paralyzed, his spirits plummeted, and at only thirty-three years of age he actually reached old age early. But the court deliberations still had to take place, the government affairs still had to be dealt with, what to do? LI Zhi, who had just brought down the autocratic prime minister, no longer trusted his courtiers, and, looking at the mountain of memos on his desk, he turned his attention to the harem.

The political spring of the Empress WU has since arrived.

Opportunity always belongs to those who are prepared. At this critical moment, the Empress WU was well educated and highly enthusiastic about politics since her childhood, which made her handy and comfortable in the process of government affairs. To his surprise, LI Zhi not only brought her to the court to listen to the government, but also “commissioned her to carry out political affairs, the power was comparable to that of the Emperor.”

However, this harmony between husband and wife did not last long, LI Zhi found that in the matter of power, he may “front door to reject a wolf, the back door to accept a tiger”, because this woman’s desire for power than any man was more than enough.

First, she was cultivating her own forces, and the first anti-ZHANGSUN figures, such as LI Yifu and XU Jingzong, had already gathered around Empress WU.

Secondly, she was secretly seizing the power of the prime minister, who had assembled a large number of famous scholars (the so-called “Bachelor of the North Gate”) to deal with various memorials under the guise of cultivating books (“Participate in decisions in order to share the prime minister’s power”).

Thirdly, she began to interface to politics: “Since then, when the emperor looked at matters, the empress would hang her curtain from the rear, and she would be informed of all matters. All the great powers were vested in the middle palace; to dethrone life and death depended on her mouth. The Son of Heaven was only arching his hand, and tast was called the Two Saints in China and abroad.”

In this whole process, I believe that LI Zhi is ambivalent: body did not allow him to shoulder all his work; if he shared his power with his empress, he could only choose the lesser of two evils, because the wife is always more trustworthy than the minister. Therefore, in the process of repetition and hesitation, the Empress WU moved step by step to the front stage. But here, I firmly believe that LI Zhi, though generous but not dim-witted, hesitant but not confused, had always grasped the measure and yardstick - but firstly, he couldn’t make the decision physically, and secondly, he underestimated WU’s desire and ambition!

In December of the first year of Hong Dao (683 A.D.), LI Zhi died at the age of fifty-six in the Zhenguan Palace in Luoyang. From then on, his history came to an end and her history began.

Illustrious civil administration, outstanding milltary achievements

A grossly underestimated prime example

As a result of the usurpation of the Tang dynasty by the Empress WU and the glory of the “Reign of Zhen Guan” and the “Age of Kai Yuan”, LI Zhi’s cultural and military achievements were completely overwhelmed. In fact, when we peel back the layers of fog and look at that period of history from the mid-seventh century, we will find that the flower of the golden age, no less than the “Zhen Guan” and “Kai Yuan”, was once bloomed splendidly during the reign of Emperor Gaozong, who was rumored to have been the weakest of all.

1.Reign of Yong Hui

Throughout the Zhen Guan period (Li Zhi’s father’s reign), although it was claimed to be in full bloom, in fact, it had been continuously pioneering territory with its amazing war power. Due to the lack of rest, the entire empire never recovered its strength. As soon as Gaozong, LI Zhi, succeeded to the throne, he immediately issued an edict, “Stop the battle of Liaodong and all civil engineering.” At the same time to comply with the policies of Zhen Guan, and listen different opinions: “if things are not convenient for the people, it is appropriate to present to the court.” In particular, he paid great attention to the suffering of the people, and successively curbed inflation by reducing coinage, controlled food prices through state purchases, and called on the people to be diligent and thrifty. Under these measures, although there were many disaster years, the national economy was still greatly recovered and developed. This period of history is called “Reign of Yong Hui”.

2. The interpretation of Tang Dynasty law

The first and most complete law in China was enacted in the fourth year of Yong Hui (653 A.D.), and its significance lies in the fact that it established the norms of ancient Chinese criminal law, from which later generations such as the Song, Yuan, Ming and Qing dynasties did not escape.

3. Vast territory

What many people do not expect is that the pinnacle of military power and national prestige of the Tang dynasty was not the Zhen Guan or the Kai Yuan, but the Gaozong dynasty. In the second year of Xian Qing, SU Dingfang defeated 100,000 Western Turkic allies at the Battle of Liqai River with 10,000 soldiers and put down the Ashina Helu Rebellion, which made the Tang Dynasty entered Central and West Asia; in the third year of Long Shuo (663), Liu Renggui defeated Japan and broke Baekje; in the first year of Zong Zhang (668), Li Shiji pacified Goryeo. By the first year of Xian Heng (670 A.D.), the imperial territory under the reign of Gao Zong had reached its fullest extent, stretching from the Korean Peninsula in the east, the Aral Sea in the west, Lake Baikal in the north, and Heng Shan in the south of Vietnam, which lasted for 32 years.

Such an emperor, who founded a thousand years of greatness, has been weakened and even discredited for thousands of years, our history is so capricious sometimes!
