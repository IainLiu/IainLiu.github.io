The Essene of Jiaozi

2020-04-18
Word count: 603   |   Reading time≈ 3 min

In the last passage, we can tell that basically ancient China’s monetary system was based on copper. But here another point emerged: How could the first paper money developed under such situation?

Jiaozi, born in the 11th century AD, was once known as the origin of world banknotes. Because the birth of Jiaozi was precisely due to the need for the convenience of commodity circulation, the government later discovered the secret of wealth accumulation, and then used power to drive it to issue arbitrarily. I also saw an interesting argument before: the advancement of papermaking and printing technology provided the prerequisites for the emergence of banknotes. Indeed, the emergence of banknotes does require paper and printing, but only these two conditions are not enough. What is more practical is the developed commodity exchange. The more important condition is that the emergency of paper money requires a mature transition of the currency standard and the development of a credit system according to the modern monetary system,. Does these two points exist when the Jiaozi is produced? No. The reason why Jiaozi was born in Sichuan at that time was only due to the inconvenience of circulation of iron and lead money in a large number of foundries at first. In this case, the birth of Jiaozi belongs to a premature economic and monetary phenomenon. As Mr. Xiao Qing said: “In the Song Dynasty… silver would soon replace copper and become the main currency in circulation, but this development process was delayed due to the emergence and widespread circulation of China’s paper money.”

Judging from the historical facts of paper currency circulation for hundreds of years since the Song and Yuan dynasties, paper currency is always in the process of evolution from the copper standard to the silver standard, which is a supplement and salvation to the copper standard. It can be said that the Tang Dynasty used silk to make up for the shortcomings of copper coins, while the Song Dynasty first used iron coins and then used Jiaozi to make up for the shortfalls of copper coins. It was not until the Qing Dynasty that the (silk) money, (jiaozi, The iron) copper money standard was transformed into the (silver) money standard. There was also a stage in which the Yuan Dynasty replaced copper and silver with banknotes and the Ming Dynasty banknotes and silver circulated in parallel. It can be seen that the production of Chinese Jiaozi is not only precocious, but also deformed. That is, it only appeared under the circumstances of Song Dynasty copper feet and iron coins, and the promotion of printing. This is obviously not the process of normal banknote production.

When the Song Dynasty first implemented Jiaozi in Shaanxi province, Shenzong Emperor revealed the secret: If it is not have no alternative, it will not issue Jiaozi; the government found that the wealth collected by issuing Jiaozi can fill the fiscal deficit effectively. The reserve fund of the Jiaozi issued by the government are still copper (iron) money. In this case, if it is fully cashed, it is not enough to solve the financial difficulties and money shortage. If it is not cashed, it will inevitably be driven by the financial will to lead the currency shortage are more serious. This can be proved by the excessive distribution of Jiaozi in the Northern Song Dynasty, the vicious expansion of Guanzi and unresolved currency shortage in the Southern Song Dynasty.

Although Jiaozi became the earliest banknote in the world, it was not advanced. We can only regard it as a complement and incarnation of the copper standard.
