A Great Speculation that Maketh a Dynasty Part 2

2021-01-24
Word count: 986   |   Reading time≈ 6 min

Follow the previous passage~

After the collapse of the Song Dynasty, the succeeding Yuan Dynasty adopted a policy of “Govern by none-interference.” But to tell the truth, the reason why Mongols aristocrats adopt such policy is not because they were sophisticated in ancient Han people’s profound thoughts–the main reason was that they were unable to build up an effective bureaucratic management system. With no effective bureaucratic team, the Yuan government was also not capable of forming a national tax system for collecting agricultural tax. So the main source of revenue of the Yuan government was business tax.

But as we know, commerce in ancient China was not as developed as it is now. For all past dynasties, the main source of tax revenue was agricultural tax. The Yuan Dynasty put the agricultural tax on the back burner, so the empire collapsed within a century.

The land is not taxed, so landlords shouldered less burden, and this made them flourish. During the Yuan Dynasty, various powerful wealthy landlords annexed large scales of land, resulting the birth of several strong capital groups–the Shanxi merchants Capital Group based on Shanxi province, Jiangnan Capital Group based on southern China, and Huainan Capital Group based on Anhui province. The Jiangnan Capital Group invested in Zhu Yuzhang in the last years of the Yuan Dynasty, and they made a right choice–Zhu Yuanzhang became the founder of the Ming Dynasty. Thanks to their wise choice, the Jiangnan Capital Group prospered greatly in the Ming Dynasty. The financial center of the Ming Dynasty was in the Jiangnan region. There was also the global financial center–tons of silvers came in everyday in exchange for tons of refined silks, tea, and porcelain.

In order to trade with the Ming Dynasty, Spain, Portugal, Holland, Japan and other countries searched for silver all over the world. With the help of Jiangnan Capital Group’s advanced ship building technology, Europeans were equipped with the world’s first-class seamanship, which indirectly promoted the geographical discovery of the Great Maritime Age.

Through speculating on Zhu Yuanzhang, the Jiangnan Capital Group obtained vigorous development in the Ming Dynasty. Two hundred yeas later, the Shanxi merchants Capital Group gained prosperous development in investing in Nurhachi, the founder of the Qing regime, in the Qing Dynasty.

There were eight royal merchants in the Qing Dynasty, all of whom were from Shanxin province. They were Fan Youdou, Wang Dengku, Jin Liangyu, King Dayu, Wang Dayu, Liang Jiabin, Tian shenglan, Zhai Tang, and Huang Yunfa.

In the late Ming and early Qing Dynasties, these eight men were veritable traitors.

There was a TV series called The Dragon Bill, which tells the story of Shanxi merchants in the Qing Dynasty. Why is the called that name? Nurhachi borrowed usury from wealthy Shanxi merchants to purchase military supplies and recruit soldiers, and the Dragon Bill was the certification of creditor’s rights stamped with Nurhachi’s seal of dragon.

After Nurhachi led her army counterattacked the Ming Dynasty, the Ming government banned anyone from sending any supplies to Nurhachi’s territory in any way. Only the Shanxi merchants who dare to risk their lives to make arduous journeys to trade with the Manchus, providing them with living and military supplies.

Before and after the Qing army entered the Shanhai Pass, these traitors, the Shanxi merchants Capital Group, offer the Qing army financial support and help them seize territories.

Of course, the Shanxi merchants Capital Group headed by those eight royal merchants had supporters in government in the Ming Dynasty, otherwise how could they conduct such smuggling with excessive profits in a low social status? (though business was prosperous in the Ming Dynasty, merchants still processed a low social status) The supporters of these Shanxi merchants was the dignitaries in the Ming government native of Shanxi province, like Yang Bo, the Secretary of Defence. The Shanxi Party supported by the Shanxi Merchants Capital Group was strong enough to compete with the Donglin Party supported by Jiangnan Capital Group. (Note: Donglin Party: an essential political group formed in 1604. Members were all from the Jiangnan region, and many of them were in really high position in the government–Prime Minister, Minister of Official Personal, etc.)

Shanxi merchants Capital Group’s strong support was the fundamental reason that the Qing Army could quickly take over northern China. With the support of Shanxi merchants Capital Group, the Manchus were able to purchase military and living supplies and recruit soldiers, making them much stronger and capable of win the battles.

The Jiangnan Capital Group in southern China and the Shanxi merchants Capital Group had been bitter rivals for a long time. The Qing army massacred wantonly in Anhui and Jiangsu provinces was in fact paying back Shanxi merchants Capital Group’s generous help. While fighting with resistance forces, the Qing army was also robbing immense amount of wealth from capitalists in Jiangnan region. Being the representative of the bureaucratic landlord class in southern China, the Donglin Party was strong in Jiangnan region. The Qing army could not get strong support from landlord class as the did in northern China, so pacifying southern China was much more difficult for them.

In this political speculation, Shanxi merchants Capital Group won a great victory, and ushered them into a golden period of development in the Qing Dynasty, leaving behind the legend of the “Silver Empire”. However, the great China was taken over by Manchus who were long considered barbarians. But in fact, though in the early and middle period of the Qing Dynasty, China was in a long prosperous time, civic life was in the worst situation–population of poor people were escalating, technological development staged and even retrogressed, and advanced thoughts were banned.

In 1644, the empire died at the hands of merchants. The merchants made considerable wealth, but the people and the culture were plunged into darkness.
