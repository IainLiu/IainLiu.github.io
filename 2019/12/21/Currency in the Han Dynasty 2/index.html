Currency in the Han Dynasty Part 2

2019-12-21
Word count: 911   |   Reading time≈ 5 min

Continued the last passage.

Less than a year after the failure of the third currency system reform, Emperor Wu of the Han Dynasty began to carry out the fourth currency system reform, since then the Half-tael coins have been banned, and then Emperor Wu began to make a brand new “Five-baht coin.” The advantage of the Five-baht coin is that it’s protected by the outer border, which makes it difficult for the copper coins to be rubbed out of copper scraps, so it can effectively prevent private casting coin. The original intention of Emperor Wu to adopt the Five-baht coin was to make a excellent Five-baht coin, and then use the protection of the outer border to make the private caster unprofitable, and solve the problem of private casting thoroughly on the premise that the civilians could recognize it.

However, since Emperor Wu didn’t take back the coinage rights of each principalities when he implemented the reform of the Five baht coins, the excellent Five-baht coins that Emperor Wu hoped did not appear in large numbers among the people. Instead, a large number of poor-quality coins from principalities appeared. This situation occurred due to the differences in the technical level of coinage and the composition of copper mines in various places at that time. In addition, the system of Five-baht coins was not unified. Sometimes there may even be some principalities Five-baht coin similar with the original Han half-tael coins–the back is flat without frame, and some have large perforations, thin or thick coin. At the same time, principalities officials often cut corners to fill their pockets when casting five baht coins, so the Five-baht coins at this time is the lighter and lighter the more it is cast, the worse and worse it the more it is cast. It can be said that it is repeating the mistake of Han Four-baht coin and Han Three-baht coin.

In order to completely solve the inferior and light-weight coinage of each principalities, Emperor Wu implemented the fifth currency reform in B.C.115. The core of this reform was to establish a Five-baht coin mint controlled by the central government in Chang’an. Which means in this year, the imperial court asked official who was in charge of casting money to start casting a fine Five-baht with a sharpened edge and purple-red color, historically known as “purple-red five-baht” coins. And this finely processed purple-red five-baht can be easily distinguished from the five-bahts made by principalities. Meanwhile, the imperial court also stipulated that 1 purple red five-baht is equivalent to 5 five-bahts made by each principalities. Then the previously issued “silver-tin alloys” currency was abolished.

However, the fifth reform was actually a failure, it’s destined to be impossible to appear on a large scale because of the fineness of the purple-red five-baht coins. In addition, although the five-baht coins from the principalities were rough, it wins in large quantities, so it’s easy to circulate on the market on a large scale. Although the purple-red five bahts was also in circulation in the hands of some people, it is five times value as much as the five bahts from the principalities, so there were also problems in the exchange. Moreover, although the cost of imitating the purple-red five bahts was relatively high, the value of it is relatively high, many private casters and pirates have started to imitate the purple-red five-baht coins.

Although the fifth currency reform was declared failure, with the increasing prestige of Emperor Wu in the entire Han Dynasty, and the central government of the Han Dynasty became stronger and stronger under the rule of Emperor Wu, the strength of various principalities was gradually weakened under the suppression of Emperor Wu for decades. Finally, when the central government of the Han Dynasty began to suppress local forces in an all-round way, Emperor Wu began the most thorough sixth currency reform. In 113 B.C., Emperor Wu ordered the prohibition each principalities from coining currencies privately, and the right of coinage was returned to the central specialized agency.

At this time, Emperor Wu completely nationalized the coinage rights, and since then the principalities in various regions could no longer privately mint copper coins. Since then, the central government has completely controlled the casting and distribution of the five-baht coin, since the five-bahts coin was then exclusively made by Shang lin san guan, the five-baht coin then was also called “Shang lin san guan officials currency” or “ san guan five bahts.” Later, with the unified manufacturing of five-baht coins, and the “Shang lin san guan officials currency” were well-made, accurate in weight, and beautiful in writing, so the cost of pirating was very high. Since then, the private casting and pirates problem which plagued the Han Dynasty for nearly a hundred years has been fundamentally solved.

Eventually, as the Han government completely controlled the issuance and casting rights of coins, the problem of the treasury emptiness during Emperor Wu’s period was also solved. Since then, the treasury of the Han Dynasty became increasingly rich, and it will be created “Xiao Xuan reviving” (74B.C.—49B.C.) soon in the future ,which was the most powerful era of the Han Dynasty.
