The Traveling Spanish Silver Part 2

*Opened the maritime trade ban, and met “maritime hegemony” Spain *

Spain has been a maritime hegemony country for a long time and has monopolized maritime trade. Since the The Great Voyage Age in the 15th century, Spain has carried out global maritime expansion and colonization, which opened the legendary era of navigation in human history.

In the middle of the 16th century, the Ming government wiped out Japanese pirates on the South-East coastal areas and opened the maritime trade ban, the so-called recovery overseas trade policies in A.D.1567. Spanish and Portuguese merchants were naturally indispensable to come to do business (Portugal has been part of Spain for a long time) with China. The Spanish used the Philippines as their base to purchase porcelain, silk and tea from China, and sell them to Europe for high profits, this is the so-called “galleon trade”, which caused a large influx of Spanish silver coins into China.

Mexico’s silver mines are inexhaustible source of silver.
Spain has an important colony in the Americas, that is Mexico. Mexico is still the world’s largest silver producer. The Saucito Silver Mine in Mexico is the largest silver mine in Mexico. This silver mine was discovered in the 16th century, at that time, silver production here accounted for a third of the world. Most Spanish silver dollars were minted here.

Bright silver was constantly excavated from the mines in Mexico and cast into inexhaustible silver coins. With the trade channel, tons and tons across half of the earth, it is widely circulated in the land of China.

Enduring for 400 years, retired in the 20th century.

Afterwards, the Spain was declined, and the maritime hegemony was successively replaced by the Netherlands and the United Kingdom. However, Spanish silver dollars were widely accepted by the Chinese public due to their early entry, high quality of fineness, and abundant output, their position as China’s common currency has not been shaken.

After defeat in the Sino-British Opium War, China’s compensation to Britain was 22 million Spanish silver dollars. After Mexico’s independence, the Spanish silver currency became the new Mexican Silver Dollar. In fact, the currency was still the currency produced there, but it was only changed a name. In order to obtain China’s financial privileges, the imperialist powers were also issued and promoted their silver coins in China, such as the “British Trade Silver coin” in the United Kingdom and the “Dragon pattern Silver coin” in Japan. The local powers also minted coins for their own interests, but these silver currencies were still at a disadvantage in the battle for common currency with the Mexican Silver Dollar.

China’s accumulated declining tendency and suffer invasion in late Qing Dynasty made the government gradually aware of the importance of financial sovereignty. “The currency from small country Mexico are flooded with domestic market”, various of silver currencies from each big powers were also circulating in domestic, led domestic financial chaos and disorder. People of insight were distressed by this.

On 10th,Aug. 1911, there was an article entitled “Reform the Currency System” in Shen Bao, (an influential newspaper at that time) which pointed out that “foreign currency could be took back if it circulated in the public.” “our country will issues new currency and unified currency system.” In 1903, Mexico announced the implementation of the gold standard and stopped casting the Mexican Silver Dollar. Since then, it has only a stock and no increase, which also created favorable conditions for China to promote the new currency. In 1914, the government of the Republic of China began to cast Yuan Shikai’s head portrait as unified silver coin“Yuan big head” coins , meanwhile repurchased and destroyed Mexican Silver Dollar. In 1919, Shanghai stopped the trading by Mexican Silver Dollar. When the Kuomintang government implemented legal currency reform in 1935, that Mexican Silver Dollar withdrew from the stage of history basically.
